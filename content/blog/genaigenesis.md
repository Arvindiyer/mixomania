## The Curious Case at a Hackathon 🤖

Last weekend I did something I had not done in **almost eight years**.

<div style="background-color: #ffe374ff; color: #000000ff; padding:16px; border-radius:8px; margin:24px 0; text-align:center;">
<strong style="color:#d62728">
I applied, got selected, and attended a <u>hackathon</u>. 🚀
</strong>
</div>

Not just any hackathon, but the **GenAI Genesis Hackathon** in Toronto, a room full of brilliant students, developers, and AI enthusiasts building wild things with LLMs, voice interfaces, and agent frameworks 🤯

And then there was me.

A **computational biology postdoc** who usually spends his days with cancer genomes and single-cell data, and occasionally being a couch potato 😅, suddenly surrounded by:

<span style="color:#9B59B6; font-weight:bold;">undergrads and even school kids speaking fluent GenAI + GenZ language</span> 🧠⚡

Let me tell you something honestly:

<span style="color:#ff7f0e; font-weight:bold;">I felt old. 😅</span>

---

## The First Shock ⚡

The vibe was electric.

Teams were forming rapidly. People were pitching ideas like:

- AI agents 🤖  
- voice-first assistants 🎙️  
- autonomous workflow engines ⚙️  
- things that sounded like startups already raising Series A 😄  

Everyone seemed to know each other or just clicked instantly. Some already had teams. Most had a **clear idea of what they wanted to build**.

Meanwhile, my brain:

> “Okay… what exactly was I thinking when I signed up for this?” 😶

For a moment:

<span style="color:#E74C3C; font-weight:bold;">Maybe I don't belong here anymore.</span>

But then something interesting happened.

<span style="color:#27AE60; font-weight:bold;">Curiosity won over insecurity.</span>

And I stayed.

**P.S.** I finally saw the Computer Science department at University of Toronto and I was like 😍 ❤️ 
Never imagined life would bring me here one day 🙈

---

## When You Can't Find a Team… Become the Team 💻🔥

I tried forming a team on **Discord** (A new medium for me!!).

A few conversations happened. Nothing concrete.

And then came a simple thought:

> If I can't find a team, I'll just become the team.

That decision changed everything.

No coordination overhead. No waiting. No alignment issues.

Just:

<span style="color:#27AE60; font-weight:bold;">pure hacker mode</span> ⚡

---

## The Idea 💡

Healthcare is something I genuinely care about. Simple, real-world ones.

Like:
<span style="color:#3498DB; font-weight:bold;">remembering medications and maintaining daily health habits</span>

So the idea became:

### MediMate — A Proactive Wellness Agent 🏥

A lightweight AI system that:

- reminds people to take medication 💊  
- tracks daily habits 📊  
- suggests simple health improvements 🥗  
- interacts conversationally 🤖💬  

The goal was not perfection.

The goal was:

> <span style="color:#E67E22; font-weight:bold;">build something real in ~36 hours</span>

---

## The Hacker Mode Experience ⚡🧠

Something magical happened when I started coding.

All the noise disappeared.

No overthinking.  
No imposter syndrome.  
Just flow.

> idea → code → test → break → fix → repeat 🔁
